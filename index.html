Principles

Understanding Problems

The key to planning is not understanding a solution but also recoginzing every aspect of a problem. Incredibly important when working with code, solutions are more than just correct and incorrect. Some are slow, fast, small, large, intractive, cooperative, neat, or messy. Know all aspects of the Project inside-out and forwards-backwards

Smooth Progression

Progress on any project is never linear no matter what team but I prioritize smooth long-term workflow over short initial burnout. Projects are completed with perseverance and problem-solving, not a 9-5.

Clean Organization

Reading code lines should be easily understood, both small and big picture. This allows for ease of cooperation, easy presentation, and cuts down on time spent trying to understand what you might've wrote weeks prior when debugging.
